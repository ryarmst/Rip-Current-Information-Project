About RiP

People should not have to learn about rip currents through tragedy.

The Rip Current Information Project translates science, lived experience, and rescue knowledge into practical information communities can use.

Why Understanding Matters

Prevention starts before anyone enters the water.

Rip currents are one of the leading causes of drowning on the Great Lakes, yet they are widely misunderstood or overlooked. Unlike dramatic ocean waves, Great Lakes rip currents can form quickly, look deceptively calm, and occur on days when the water appears inviting.

Understanding when, where, and why rip currents occur empowers people to make safer decisions before entering the water. It also helps communities, emergency responders, and decision-makers move from reactive rescue to proactive prevention.

The Story Behind This Project

Born from personal loss, lived experience, and preventable gaps.

The Rip Current Information Project was born out of personal loss, lived experience, and a growing frustration with preventable tragedies. After witnessing firsthand how little accurate, accessible information exists about Great Lakes rip currents, and how often warnings come too late, this project was created to close that gap.

What began as one person asking hard questions has grown into a collaborative effort focused on translating science into practical, life-saving information. Clear communication, shared knowledge, and early awareness can change outcomes.

Who We Serve

Everyone who interacts with the Great Lakes.

We serve residents, visitors, families, anglers, paddlers, swimmers, and beachgoers of all ages. We also support first responders, municipalities, educators, and community organizations by providing reliable information that can inform safety messaging, policy, and on-the-ground decision-making.

At our core, we serve people who may never realize they were at risk. If someone goes home safely because they understood the conditions that day, our work has done its job.

Founder & CEO

Nathan MacIntyre

Nathan MacIntyre is a water safety advocate whose work is rooted in lived experience, community engagement, and collaboration with coastal scientists and emergency responders.

He founded the Rip Current Information Project to address gaps in public understanding around Great Lakes rip currents and to promote prevention-focused safety messaging. Nathan is available for media interviews, expert commentary, and public education discussions related to rip currents, beach safety, and drowning prevention.
