Speaking Requests

Nathan MacIntyre is available for speaking engagements related to rip current rescue, science, awareness, Great Lakes risk and prevention, lifeguarding expertise, and community education.

The "Understanding Rip Currents" presentation is approximately 60 minutes and can be tailored for conferences, community events, municipal meetings, beach sessions, and public awareness initiatives.

Please include

  • Event name and location.
  • Preferred date, format, and audience size.
  • Audience type and any local water safety concerns.

School Inquiries

Schools and educators interested in free water safety education are encouraged to get in touch.

  • School name and location.
  • Grade level or levels.
  • Preferred format: classroom or beach-based.
  • Preferred timing.

Municipalities & Local Governments

Municipal presentations focus on local risk factors, facility audits, standardized language, proactive beach management, and alignment across departments and partners.

Rescue Agencies & Responders

Sessions emphasize rip current science, how structures influence rescue risk, prevention messaging, and responder safety through public education.

The Rip Current Information Project is a Canadian, Ontario-based public safety initiative focused on reducing drowning risk on the Great Lakes by improving awareness of rip current occurrence and conditions.

Media-Ready Quotes

  • "Most people don't realize rip currents happen on the Great Lakes. That misunderstanding is a risk factor."
  • "The most important safety decision happens before you enter the water. Checking weather conditions saves lives."
  • "You don't need to be an expert to stay safe. You just need the right information at the right time."
  • "Rip currents are strongest at the surface. Rips pull you away from shore. Rips do not pull you under the water."
