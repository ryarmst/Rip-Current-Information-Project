For Families & Beachgoers

A beach day should be fun, relaxing, and safe.

Understanding basic water safety helps families enjoy the Great Lakes while reducing risk, especially when conditions change quickly.

Simple Safety Tips

  • Check the weather, wind, and water conditions before you go.
  • Be cautious on windy days or after storms, even if the sun is out.
  • Stay well away from piers, breakwalls, and jetties.
  • Swim near lifeguards when they are present.
  • If conditions do not feel right, choose not to enter the water.

What Parents Should Know

Rip currents occur on the Great Lakes and can be difficult to spot. Dangerous currents may exist even when the water looks calm or after a storm passes.

Children can be pulled into deeper water very quickly. Many emergencies happen close to shore, near structures, or in deeper areas around sandbars.

Talking to Kids

Children do not need scary details. They need clear, simple rules.

  • "We check the water conditions before we swim."
  • "If the water is rough or the waves are big, we stay out."
  • "If you feel tired or pulled away from shore, float and wave for help."

Beach Checklist

Do not let your first safety check happen in the lake.

A few minutes of preparation can prevent a dangerous or deadly situation. Local knowledge can be helpful, but conditions should still be checked before anyone enters the water.

Before You Go

  • Review the weather and wind forecast.
  • Pay attention to wind direction and strength, not just temperature.
  • Be extra cautious near piers, breakwalls, and jetties.
  • Observe flags, signs, advisories, and closures.
  • Decide where you will swim before entering the water.
  • Talk with family or friends about what to do if someone is pulled away.
  • Call 911 and throw or extend something that floats if someone is in trouble.
  • When conditions feel confusing, stay out of the water.
