---
title: "Rip Current Lessons Visit Lakeview Public School"
date: 2026-04-18
author: "Nathan MacIntyre"
excerpt: "A recent classroom visit helped students connect Great Lakes water safety with the beaches they know."
---

Students at Lakeview Public School recently welcomed the Rip Current Information Project for an age-appropriate water safety presentation focused on Great Lakes conditions, rip current awareness, and simple decisions that can reduce risk before anyone enters the water.

The session introduced students to the idea that freshwater beaches can still have powerful currents. Students learned how wind, waves, sandbars, and shoreline structures can influence conditions, and why calm-looking water is not always safe.

The most important message was simple: check conditions before swimming, stay near supervised areas when possible, and float if you ever feel tired or pulled away from shore.

RiP is continuing to develop classroom presentations, student engagement activities, curriculum-aligned learning experiences, rip current dioramas, children's books, and more. Schools interested in a free visit can contact <a href="mailto:RipSafety1@gmail.com">RipSafety1@gmail.com</a>.
