Water Safety Education

Education that meets people where they are.

Effective water safety education saves lives when it is age-appropriate, evidence-informed, and delivered by people who understand the real mechanisms.

Age-Appropriate Learning

RiP is creating education that aligns with developmental stages and existing safety frameworks, including Grade 3 curriculum concepts and programs such as Swim to Survive.

  • Younger learners focus on recognizing unsafe water conditions.
  • Students learn when not to enter the water.
  • Lessons introduce basic self-rescue principles in clear language.

Older Students & Adults

For older students and adults, education expands into identifying rip current risk factors and understanding how weather, wind, waves, and structures influence water conditions.

Our goal is not to create fear, but to build confidence through knowledge and understanding.

Classroom & Beach-Based Learning

Classroom learning builds the foundation. Beach-based learning helps participants observe real conditions, identify hazards, and practice decision-making in a safe, supervised environment.

Evidence-Informed Messaging

Public education should be accurate, practical, and local.

All RiP educational content is grounded in current research and real-world coastal science. We work closely with leading experts to ensure information shared with the public is accurate, practical, and relevant to the Great Lakes.

This includes collaboration with Dr. Chris Houser, Dean of Science at the University of Waterloo and a leading authority on rip current dynamics. By sharing evidence-informed messaging presented by Great Lakes lifeguards who have responded to dangerous current emergencies, RiP helps bridge science and public safety.
